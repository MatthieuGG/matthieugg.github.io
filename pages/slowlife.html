TAKE BACK CONTROL
GO SLOW LIFE
//////////////////////////////////////

Technological tools and services are conceptualised to be attractive. They are colorfull, as convenient as smart, easy to use and to access. You get addicted by design.

What is presented to you through social networks is a selected and distorted reality. You are facing distorting mirrors and confirmation bias.

Internet never ends. There will always be new trends, new content. You get stuck in the culture of the moment.

We cannot simultaneously live in the present, archive it and consume the past. You are already loosing a rat race.

We live in imposed overexposure to media hubbub. You deserve digital silence - and you will always be informed of important things.

It is more difficult each day to focus on the same task even for a period. Youre attention capacities are melting.

The more you rest, the more you feel exhausted. Your are lacking of power, and everything you tentatively do to gain some ends the opposite.

The easiest way to control work/personnal balance is to be efficient. You should aim at reduicing your working hours as much as possible, rather than achieve more.

So, what to do?

  1. Bring back the power of borness. You need to do nothing to put your brain to rest, think, and be creative.
  2. (re)discover the power of handwritting and taking notes
  3. Print to read
  4. Prefer phonecalls to texts
  5. Merely nothing needs to be done in the minute
  6. Distinguish priority from urgency
  7. "Less is more" - do less, do better
  8. Cut the hyperproductivity nonsense race
  9. Turn your phone black and white
  10. Use a paper agenda
  11. By a dumb wrist watch
  12. Leave your phone outside of the bedroom, and don't put it on your desk
  13. Avoid multi tasking - always do one thing at a time
  14. Avoid easy and convenient solutions, such as bluetooth earplugs or x2 speed
  15. Cut your wifi when you're done
  16. Use timer limitation on your apps
  17. Use an old-school FM in your toilets and bathroom
  18. Never consult passively the never-ending scroling plateforms
  19. Use the "3,2,1" hack to start tasks
  20. Start your working day with the hardest task
  21. End your working day with an easy win
  22. First, define a duration for any task
  23. Only use social networks for duffision, never consultation
  24. Stop screens 1h before going to bed, and read before sleeping
  25. Sleep early, wake up early
  26. Do any physical activity you like, as much as you can
  27. Eat well, slowly, and drink as much water as you can

Those are just advices from my own experience. If you want references, books and studies, there are more than you can ever read. Test, adjust, do your own thing. Be curious and critical.
