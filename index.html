Bio

I am a French researcher with a keen interest in sports science and neuroscience. As a former physical therapist, I have a background in clinical research and have since shifted my focus towards data-driven research (Python, MATLAB). Curious and mobile, I have lived and worked in France, Canada and Japan. Animal lover, I am also a sports enthusiast, particularly for outdoor activities and martial arts.

Research

My research journey began in 2019. Since then, I have been dedicated to scientific valorization and communication, emphasizing the importance of engaging both specialized audiences and the broader public through popularization efforts. I have published articles, shared scripts and data, presented at conferences, secured funding and grants, and actively promoted scientific understanding. My past and current projects include:

  • Studying the impact of different stimuli through encoding and decoding models from brain activity (fMRI - ongoing)
  • Time series analysis between physical activity and clinical events in sickle cell anemia patients (drePAnon)
  • Developping an exergame for fall prevention in older adults (INCOME)

Teaching

Since 2020, I have been teaching undergraduate and graduate students in sports science and physiotherapy, focusing on topics such as geriatrics, clinical practice, and research methodology. I believe in the need for active learning and the importance of hands-on, focused and project-based education. I have also supervised several master's theses. These experiences led to my certification as a lecturer in rehabilitation, sports science, and neuroscience by the French National University Council (CNU) in 2023.
